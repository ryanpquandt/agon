# Voice corpus

Drop your past publications here so Agon can learn your voice — op-eds, essays,
papers, book chapters, talks, anything you've written and would want to sound
like. Plain text, Markdown, PDF, or `.docx` all work; a mix of genres is ideal,
since voice shifts between an op-ed and a journal article and Agon should know
both registers.

## How to populate it

- **Just copy files in** (any of the formats above), or
- Ask Agon to help pull them from Google Drive or elsewhere, or
- Paste text into a new `.md` file here.

Optional: group by register in subfolders, e.g. `scholarly/`, `essays/`,
`op-eds/`. Not required.

## Building the profile

Once files are in, run **`agon-voice`** (or just say "build my voice profile").
It analyzes the corpus and writes `voice-profile.md`: the characteristic moves
worth amplifying *and* the recurring tics worth leaving behind.

## The two rules of voice in Agon

1. **Ground in the corpus.** Keep the writing recognizably yours.
2. **Surpass its infelicities.** The profile documents your weaker habits on
   purpose — so the next piece can be the *best* version of your voice, not a
   faithful copy of past ones. Agon is never constrained to repeat what didn't
   work.
